MONEY QUESTIONS

Where does my money go after I make my donation?
  • Your money, by credit card or by check, is transferred into ETF’s U.S. bank account and is then transferred via MoneyGram to the church leader who submitted the need profile that you have selected. The church leader receives and forwards the support to the right individual or family.

Do the people who manage ETF earn a salary?
  • The board members and volunteers of ETF do not receive any compensation for their time. As the site grows, some of the volunteers doing data entry and managing local relationships may in the future receive a reasonable salary.

What percentage of my donation goes to the individual or family I select?
  • 100%. Operational fees are not removed from your gift.  And fees related to transferring funds internationally are not removed from your gift.

Why is $50 a month for one year, or $600 in total, the only support option provided?
  • The ETF program is very new, and in the future may expand to include other giving options.
  • By choosing one giving level, it is much easier to create and upload need profiles. Donations at this level may meet a need for six months, or for an entire year. This is made clear in the need profile.
  • Groups of people are encouraged to register together and select a need together.

How is ETF funded?
  • The operational costs of ETF are covered by a number of generous individual disciples who believe in the program. These operational costs include the fees charged by MoneyGram, so 100% of the support given by ETF members reaches the intended individuals or families.
  • Donations from individuals or groups who have registered at ETF are used to meet the specific needs that are presented on the site.

Is my donation tax deductible?
  • Yes. ETF is a registered not-for-profit 501c3 organization, which means your donation is tax deductible in the United States. You will receive a receipt for your donation after you register.
 

PROGRAM QUESTIONS

Who manages ETF?
  • A small group of disciples make up the board of directors. We have chosen not to put our names on the website; however if you wish to learn more about who we are you may email us at info@especiallythefamily.org.

What does ETF do with my personal and financial information?
  • The ETF database only stores your name, physical address and email address. The ETF web site uses a leading ecommerce provider that uses industry-best practices to safeguard your financial data. The staff of ETF have no access at any time to your credit card information.

How does ETF decide where the money I donate goes?
  • After you commit to registering with ETF, you will be given the option to identify which needs you would like your support to meet. In the event that the need has already been met, or has been removed from the site, the staff of ETF may ask you to select another need. You may also designate upon registration that you would like the staff of ETF to select a need profile on your behalf.

Does ETF support only ICOC members?
  • ETF is committed to supporting people of Christian faith. At the present time we are focusing our efforts on members of the International Churches of Christ throughout the world as they represent the people we are most familiar with.

Can only ICOC members join and give to ETF?
  • Anyone can join and meet a need at the ETF site.

 

CONFIDENTIALITY QUESTIONS

Can I pick the specific person that receives my support?
  • Yes. After you have registered and committed to funding a need, you will then be able to choose the specific need that you would like to meet.

Will the person who receives my support know who I am?
  • No, your giving will be anonymous. However, you will receive a thank you letter within a month or two and at that time you may choose to initiate communication with the recipient of your donation. You will remain anonymous unless you decide to send additional communications.

Are the identities of the people who receive support through ETF kept confidential?
  • Yes and no.
  • Visitors to the ETF site who have not registered and made a donation cannot see any confidential information.
  • ETF members who have completed the registration process may log in and see the need profiles of individuals and families that have an unselected need profile on the site. In these need profiles, first names may be provided. These individuals or families are asked to agree to this when they work with their local church leader to submit their need profile.
  • Once a need has been met, the need profile is removed from the site. ETF members will only learn the full identity of the people whose needs they have met when they receive a thank you letter after one or two months.
